Definition: The mean is the sum of all values divided by the number of values.

Example: (4 + 6 + 8) / 3 = 6

Definition: The median is the middle number in a sorted list of values.

Example: For [3, 5, 7], the median is 5.

Definition: A z-score tells you how many standard deviations a value is from the mean.

Formula: z = (x - μ) / σ

Example: A z-score of 2 means the value is 2 standard deviations above the mean.

Definition: Variance measures the average squared deviation from the mean.

Formula: σ² = Σ(x - μ)² / N

Interpretation: High variance means data is spread out.

Definition: Correlation measures the strength and direction of a linear relationship between two variables.

Range: -1 to +1.

Example: Height and weight are positively correlated.

Definition: Skewness tells you whether the distribution of data leans to the left or right.

Types: Positive skew (right tail), Negative skew (left tail).

Machine Learning (ML) is how we teach computers to learn from data — without being explicitly programmed.

Instead of giving it instructions, we give it examples. The machine finds patterns and learns how to make decisions on its own.

📘 Types of Machine Learning:

  • Supervised Learning: Learns from labeled data (input + known output).
    Example: Spam detection, price prediction.
  • Unsupervised Learning: Discovers patterns in unlabeled data.
    Example: Customer segmentation, topic discovery.
  • Reinforcement Learning: Learns by taking actions and receiving rewards.
    Example: Game AIs, robotics.

🔁 How It Works:

  1. Collect Data 📊
  2. Preprocess & Clean Data 🧹

🔧 Popular Algorithms:
