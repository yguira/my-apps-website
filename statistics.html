Definition: The mean is the sum of all values divided by how many values.

Example: (10 + 20 + 30) / 3 = 20

Use: Useful for finding central tendency.

Definition: The middle value in a sorted list.

Example: Sorted: [1, 3, 5] → Median = 3

Why it matters: Not affected by outliers.

Definition: The value(s) that appear most often.

Example: [2, 2, 3, 3, 3, 4] → Mode = 3

Definition: Difference between highest and lowest values.

Formula: Max - Min

Example: [2, 4, 7] → Range = 7 - 2 = 5

Definition: How far each number is from the mean (on average).

Formula: σ² = Σ(x - μ)² / N

Example: A low variance means data is close to the mean.

Definition: The square root of variance; shows data spread.

Use: Common in everything from finance to ML models.

Definition: How many standard deviations a value is from the mean.

Formula: z = (x - μ) / σ

Example: z = (85 - 70) / 10 = +1.5

Definition: Measures the asymmetry of a distribution.

  • ➡️ Positive skew: tail on the right (e.g. income)
  • ⬅️ Negative skew: tail on the left

Definition: Measures relationship strength between two variables.

Range: -1 to +1

Example: +0.9 means strong positive correlation

Definition: A range of values that's likely to contain a population parameter.

Example: "We're 95% confident the mean is between 68 and 72."

What is it? Method to test if a claim (hypothesis) about a population is true.

  • Null Hypothesis (H₀): Default assumption
  • Alternative Hypothesis (H₁): What you're testing for
  • Use: t-tests, chi-square tests, p-values

Definition: The probability of observing your results, assuming H₀ is true.

Rule: If p < 0.05 → reject H₀
